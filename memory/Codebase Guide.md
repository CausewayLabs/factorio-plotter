## Invariants

*Things that must always hold. Promote load-bearing decisions here from feature log completed entries — do not wait for a prune cycle.*

- **Authored vs. derived geometry is a hard boundary.** Rails and bubbles are *authored* (persistent, user-placed, attachable, forkable). Feeders **and output connectors** are *derived* (recomputed by the layout solver from current geometry, never selectable, never an attachment target). You may only attach to / fork off authored objects.
- **Rails are immune to bubble movement, full stop.** A rail's shape changes only when the user edits it; moving a bubble re-runs the solver but never moves a rail. Rails are **never anchored to bubbles** — there is no `bubbleOrigin`. A bubble's output reaches a bus through a *derived* output connector (see next invariant), not by gluing the rail's endpoint to the bubble. The only inter-rail anchoring that exists is a **tee** on its parent rail: `Rail.tee?: { parentRailId, anchorEndIndex: 0 | 1 }`. A tee child stores its **free endpoint** at `points[0]` and a **ray-direction reference** at `points[1]` (never rendered). At resolve time the resolver casts a ray from `points[0]` toward `points[1]` and intersects it with the parent's resolved polyline: **hit** → junction = first intersection; **miss** → orthogonal L into parent endpoint #`anchorEndIndex`. Because authored rails are H/V, the ray and the parent's endpoint tangent are perpendicular, so the elbow is always finite and 90° — the child stays connected even when the parent slides past the ray, and re-snaps automatically when the parent comes back over it. `resolveRailPolyline(rail, rails)` in `scene/geometry.ts` is the single resolver used by renderer, hit-testing, endpoint editing, and the solver; never resolve a tee inline. **`anchorEndIndex` is updated by drag handlers only** (via `recomputeTeeAnchors(parentId)` in the scene store) whenever the ray currently hits the parent — the resolver itself stays a pure function of stored state. *(Replaced the percentage-`t` parametric model 2026-05-26: storing the fork as a fraction of parent length caused the junction to scale with parent length and skew when the parent's endpoint moved; the ray model anchors by direction, which is invariant under parent reshaping.)* *(Superseded the bubble-origin carve-out 2026-05-24: an anchored rail forced a choice between sloping geometry and re-pinned length on every drag; modeling the output as a derived connector — the mirror of a feeder — eliminates the dilemma and keeps rails purely orthogonal authored geometry.)*
- **A bubble emits its output onto a bus via an explicit binding + derived connector.** The bubble carries `outputTarget: railId | null`. When set: (1) the bubble's product is present in that rail's `resourceTypes` (so downstream feeders can tap it off the bus — the rail's `resourceTypes` stays the single source of truth for "what's on a bus"), and (2) the solver draws a derived orthogonal **output connector** from `bubbleOutputPort` to the nearest point on the resolved rail. The connector re-routes when either the bubble or the rail moves and is always 90° (unlike feeders, which are direct). Dragging out from the output port and **dropping on a rail** sets the binding and adds the product to that rail; **dropping on empty space** creates a new single-resource rail carrying the product and binds to it. The rail itself is always free, ordinary, orthogonal authored geometry.
- **Rails are buses: each carries one or more resource types** (`Rail.resourceTypes: string[]`, ≥1; optional `label` names a multi-type bus). A plain rail is a bus of one. The solver attaches a feeder to a rail when the needed resource is in `resourceTypes` (`rail.resourceTypes.includes(...)`). Bundled multi-resource presets live in `recipes/sources.ts` (e.g. the Fulgora **scrap** bus = the 12 direct recycling outputs); users add/remove materials per-rail via the rail context menu. *(Supersedes the original "one resource per rail, no multi-resource buses" invariant — deliberately changed 2026-05-23 to model Space Age recyclers.)*
- **Authored rails are orthogonal.** Every rail segment is horizontal or vertical (90°). The draw tool snaps each committed vertex and the live preview to H/V relative to the previous vertex (`orthoSnap` in `Canvas.tsx`); diagonal rail segments are never authored. (Matches the orthogonal feeder routing — the whole graph reads as a bus layout.)
- **No collision avoidance.** Feeder/rail routing is purely geometric and may cross any bubble, rail, or feeder. The solver never routes around obstacles; layout cleanliness is the user's responsibility (drag bubbles to tidy).
- **Product identity is matched by canonical key, never by raw string equality.** Two product/resource names refer to the same thing when their `canonicalProductKey` (`recipes/normalize.ts` — case-folded, hyphens/underscores/whitespace stripped) is equal. Anywhere the system resolves a *needed* resource to a *source* — the solver's rail/bubble source search, `getResourceColor`, and Recipe Editor ingredient resolution — compare canonical keys, not `===`. Canonicalization is a matching aid only: stored ids and labels are never rewritten (no persistence migration). New ingredients enter through forced-match autocomplete (`resolveProductId`) with a deliberate "create" escape for genuinely new names.
- **Bubble port geometry is a single source of truth.** `bubbleInputPort`/`bubbleInputBox` and `bubbleOutputPort` (in `scene/geometry.ts`) are consumed by *both* the renderer (`BubbleLayer`) and the solver (the input port `=` the feeder's endpoint). Never compute port positions independently in either place — change the geometry helper and both stay in sync. `bubbleInputBox(center, side, index, total)` is **side-aware**: tabs sit on the left or right and stagger outward, with `port` = the outer-edge feeder attach and `innerX` = the edge toward the bubble.
- **Input tab side is solver-derived, not renderer-derived.** Which side (left/right) each input tab snaps to depends on the direction its feeder arrives from (source left of the bubble center → left tab; right → right tab; the vertical line through the center is the "12:00" flip boundary; unmet inputs default left). The solver computes this — finding each source by querying from the bubble **center** (side-agnostic, which avoids the port↔source circular dependency) — and publishes it as `inputLayouts: Record<bubbleId, InputSlot[]>` in derived store state. The renderer **reads** `inputLayouts` (falling back to all-left before the first solve) rather than recomputing the side. Per-side indices come from the shared `assignSideIndices` helper. Because the solver reruns on every bubble move, tabs flip sides live as a source sweeps across 12:00.
